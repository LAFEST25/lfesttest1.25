HARMONY
Where all hushes succumb to noise, and all pitches are forged from the breath of life. For the ones who believe every beat, every breath, and every bar is a part of who they are, Harmony is not a contest - it's a battle. Where expression finds its voice, the stage comes alive... Where voices will be heard and respected in a new way that extends beyond the time on stage and forever changes what greatness should sound like.
RULES
1. Each school may send only one team, of 8 to 16 participants; their performance must not exceed 8 minutes.
2. Teams are free to choose any soundtrack or song for their performance, but the selected soundtrack must be submitted to the event heads via the phone numbers provided on the website at least two days prior to the event.
3. The use of party poppers, explosive items, powdery substances and water or any liquid on stage is strictly prohibited.
4. USB drives are mandatory and must be brought by the participants and handed over to the event heads on the day of the fest.
5. Special lighting effects will not be provided. Obscenity or vulgarity, in either lyrics or actions, will lead to immediate disqualification.
6. All performance sequences must take place entirely on stage.
7. If a team chooses to present a theme, it must be clearly conveyed to both the judges and the audience. Judges' decisions are final and binding.
8. Participants are encouraged to use small, manageable props, which must be carried and handled by the performers themselves.
9. All props must be cleared from the stage immediately after the performance.
10. While ushers may assist, teams are solely responsible for managing their own props.
11. Failure to comply with Rules 2,3 and 4 will result in deduction of points.

RULES

RULES
1. Each school may nominate a maximum of four contestants to participate in this event.
2. The event comprises three rounds:
    • Glance - Written round
    • Gaze - Interview round
    • The Unveil - Final onstage round
3. All participants are expected to show up in modest attire.
4. Strict adherence to time limits for each segment is mandatory. Any deviation may result in penalties, including point deductions.
5. Any form of vulgarity, obscenity, or the use of unparliamentary language is strictly prohibited. Such infractions will result in immediate point deductions or potential disqualification.
6. The judges' decision will be final and irrevocable.
7. La Persona will crown only one winner.

RULES
1. Each school may send a team comprising two members.
2. Performance in the prelims will determine entry into the semi-finals and finals.
3. Prelims rules: Speakers will be allowed one minute to deliver their points, after which the opposition can ask questions for a maximum of two minutes. A maximum of three questions is allowed.
4. Finals rules: The finals will be centred around a moot court scenario. Participants will not be allowed to change their stand during the debate.
5. During the rebuttal session, the speaker is not allowed to seek the help of his teammate, and cross-questioning is not allowed. No new arguments may be introduced in the rebuttal period.
6. Details regarding the semi-finals and finals will be provided on the day of the event.

RULES
1. Each school may register up to six teams for the preliminary round.
2. Each team must consist of only two members.
3. The prelims will feature twenty questions to determine the finalists.
4. Only one team per school can qualify for the finals.
5. In case of a tie, starred questions will be used as tiebreakers.
6. The top six teams will advance to the final round.
7. The finals may include question formats such as Pounce and Bounce, Buzzer, or Rapid Fire.
